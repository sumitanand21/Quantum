Competitor guidelines

Using the Competitor Grid

The Competitor Grid is a central place where you can record the likely competitors for your Opportunity and track their progress, together with Wipro’s progress, through the sales stages.

Competitors identified in the Competitor Grid also flow into the Competitor Strategy Tool (CST) which you can find in the Opportunity Tool Kit. Similarly, competitors that are newly defined in the CST will flow back to the Competitor Grid so that both are kept in sync.

Adding Competitors

When you open the Competitor Grid you will see that three entries are predefined. These are ‘Wipro’, ‘In House’ and ‘Do Nothing’. While it may be obvious that ‘Wipro’ is a competitor in this Opportunity, we need the entry so that in the CST we can set a strategy for any of our strengths or weaknesses. ‘In House’ and ‘Do Nothing’ are there to remind you that these options are always serious ‘competitors’ for your Opportunity. In the CST you will also be able to set strategies for these ‘competitors’.

Use the ‘search’ symbol to open up the list of competitors and select the competitor you need. As you add a competitor, a new blank row appears ready for you to add the next competitor

Ranking Competitors

Competitors are automatically ranked in the order that you add them. The first one added is ranked #1, the second #2 and so on. You can change the ranking by highlighting the competitor row and using the ‘move up’ or ‘move down’ button.

Note that ‘Wipro’, ‘In House’ and ‘Do Nothing’ have a fixed ranking so they cannot be moved up or down.

Incumbent Competitors

You can mark any of the competitors (including Wipro but not ‘In House’ or ‘Do Nothing’) as an incumbent. An incumbent is a Third Party that is already providing services to the Account.

As soon as you mark a competitor as incumbent, the Current Scope Share field open up. Here you can record the (estimated) share of revenue that this competitor has of the current Opportunity(not of the overall Account).

Tracking Progress

As you move through the sales cycle, new fields will be added to the grid so you can mark the progress of the competitors in the sales cycle. These are fields that you just need to tick against each competitor (see below) Note that ‘Wipro’ will be auto-ticked as you move from one sales stage to the next.

Longlisted?

Here you can mark all competitors that have been ‘Long-listed’ which means they have also been invited to the buying process.

Shortlisted?

Here you can mark all competitors that have been Short-listed. You can mark them both in the case where Wipro is also Shortlisted or where Wipro was eliminated.

Shared Win?

Use this field to mark any competitor that also won part of the scope of the overall Opportunity. You should mark this in case the customer awards part of the scope we bid for to another party as well as if certain work was awarded to another party that we did not bid for, but was in our normal area of offerings.

When you mark any competitor with ‘Shared Win’ a new field will open up where you can record the share of the win, in percentage terms.

Winning Party

In the case where Wipro lost, you can indicate which of the competitors won the deal. This can be one or more. When there are multiple winners, you can indicate the share of the revenue won for each competitor.

Updating the Competitor Grid

Note that you can update the Competitor Grid at any time, even after the Opportunity has been hard-closed, as information may not be available at the time of closing the Opportunity. Please record as much information as you can as this will help us to analyse our competitive position and performance and learn to improve the way we compete.

Team building guidelines

The opportunity team building panel lets you do the following:

  • Assign a role to a named person.
  • Request a resource for a specific role.
  • Identify a role and start/ end details, but not fulfil it for now (make a memo note).

Each of these options is detailed below.

Assign a role to a named person

When you have a name of the person that will be part of the Opportunity Team, you can tag that person to the team. You will have to:

  • Enter the name.
  • Assign a role from the list of available roles.
  • Select a start date.
  • Select an end date.
  • Add some details.

When you save the data, an email is automatically generated to that person to notify that he or she is now part of the team. The email will include the role name, start and end date and the information you added to the ‘Additional Detail’ box.

Use the ‘Additional Detail’ box to outline what you expect this person to do, how to get in contact, etc.

Request a resource for a specific role

This option allows you to request a resource. Use this if you need a person for a specific role, but do not have a name. This will also automatically generate an email, with the details as above, but the email will be sent to the Pre-sales Head of the unit to which the opportunity is tagged.

Note that ‘Wipro’, ‘In House’ and ‘Do Nothing’ have a fixed ranking so they cannot be moved up or down.

Here also you will have a box for ‘Additional Detail’ but this time the details are used to communicate with the Pre-sales Head so you may wish to state the urgency, minimum requirements or specific knowledge that you need from the resource.

Identify a role and start/ end details

Use this option to start identifying the required roles and their anticipated start & end date. This will serve as a ‘memo’ but there is no action from the system at this point.

About ‘Process Roles’

The list of roles that are available to choose from are mainly Process Roles with some Positional Roles.

‘Process Roles’ are roles that signify the roles and responsibilities for the opportunity. For example, the role of ‘Bid Owner’ may be assigned to a person for this particular opportunity only. This person can have a totally different ‘Positional Role’, for example, ‘PreSales Consultant’ or ‘Engagement Manager’

One person can have one or more process roles, but certain process roles cannot be duplicated. These include ‘Pursuit Lead’, ‘Business Solution Owner’, ‘Executive Sponsor’, ‘Pricing Lead’ etc.

Probability guidelines

Opportunity Management has three different sets of probabilities:

  • System Probability.
  • StormCast Probability.
  • Manual Probability.

Each of these terms is explained below.

System Probability

This is a probability which is automatically assigned to the Opportunity based on the sales stage it is in. It is expressed as a term, rather than a percentage, which will be converted in certain financial reports to a percentage. Upside/ Commit

The ‘Manual Probability’ (as well as the ‘Estimated Closure Date’) has an effect on both the ‘Upside’ and ‘Commit’ function as the functions will only be enabled if the ‘Manual Probability’ has a minimum value as follows:

  • For the ‘Upside’ to be activated, the ‘Manual Probability’ must be >=10% and the ‘Estimated Closure Date’ must be within 180 days.
  • For the ‘Commit’ to be activated, the ‘Manual Probability’ must be >=75% and the ‘Estimated Closure Date’must be within 180 days.
